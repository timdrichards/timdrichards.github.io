Teaching Philosophy

As an educator, I firmly believe that all students can learn and be successful, and I am dedicated to developing teaching strategies that support the learning of all students. My instruction is tailored to the needs of each student, and I strive to create an environment in which every learner can achieve their goals. I have developed a variety of methods and tools to ensure that all students can access and benefit from my instruction, including using multi-sensory activities, allowing for flexibility in assignments and pacing, leveraging technology, and providing personalized instruction. I am passionate about helping all learners reach their full potential.

In my heart I am a teacher, and I take great joy in helping students to learn. I love the learning process imparting knowledge to help students grow, both academically and personally. I have been blessed with the opportunity to impact the lives of many students, and for that I am thankful. With my passion for learning, I strive to create a stimulating and rewarding environment for my students. I believe in inspiring them to discover the world around them and develop the skills to become successful, contributing members of society. Being able to take something complex and make it easier for my students to understand, or watching them have an ah-ha moment, brings me so much joy. I strive to be a teacher who ignites a passion for learning, not just for my students but also in myself.

My teaching methodology is premised on the power, contributions, and learning of all peoples. To create an inclusive and equitable teaching environment, it is important to incorporate assignments and projects that recognize the diversity of my students in both their presentation and context. This means bringing in a welcoming and inclusive voice and emphasizing that each voice in the classroom has something of value to contribute. Designing lectures and classroom activities that are accessible to all students by using inclusive language and topics is crucial. To gather feedback on the classroom experience through surveys that allow for student input and reflection is a critically important communication tool that can be used to direct the course climate and delivery and give students ownership over the material. Furthermore, a flexible learning environment should be provided for students to acknowledge that we all learn differently, and that life can intervene in ways that are beyond our control. I do create an inclusive and equitable teaching environment which has been communicated to me in a variety of ways by my students in both their recognition, appreciation, and success in my courses.

As an effective teacher, I strive to connect my teaching goals to student learning. To achieve this, I align my teaching methods to the objectives of my lesson plans, and measure student learning using a variety of assessments including quizzes, exercises, projects, and reflections to ensure that these objectives are being met. Additionally, I emphasize the importance of positive attitudes to help students become successful in computer science. This includes a focus on problem solving, persistence, collaboration, and creative thinking. By doing so, I hope to empower my students to make the most of their computer science experience and bring to the world their own unique creativity and background to solve real-world problems.

An excellent teacher is firm but kind, providing homework and projects emphasizing learner-centered activities that are attuned to individual student differences. They can effectively design quizzes and exams to be formative rather than summative assessments that measure understanding and student progress. They understand the importance of student reflection in the learning process, which is key to long-lasting learning and knowledge. Finally, what makes an excellent teacher even more remarkable, perhaps distinguished, is the way their energy rubs off on the students, making them want to learn as much as the teacher wants them to. I believe I am a distinguished teacher when I reflect on the many words of my students, their successes, and the acknowledgement of my collogues.

I have always been passionate about teaching, and my deep love of learning has enabled me to make a real impact on the lives of many students. My passion for knowledge has allowed me to build a comprehensive understanding of the subjects I teach, so I can provide the best possible instruction for my students. I am not only capable of accomplishing an excellent teaching and learning environment for a class of 10 students, but I am just as effective at doing so for large classes with 300 or more. I believe that this is very much what distinguishes me from the many other excellent teachers I am so lucky to work with and work hard every day to continue to innovate in my teaching to ensure the success of all my students.
